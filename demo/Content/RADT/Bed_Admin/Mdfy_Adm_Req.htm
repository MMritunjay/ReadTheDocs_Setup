    1. Modify Admission Request

    You can register new and follow-up patients using the Amrita HMIS Registration interface.Patients must be first registered and an MRD number assigned to them in the AHMIS system before they can see the doctor or be admitted into the hospital. The MRD number is a sequential number generated for every patient after registration.

    Since the MRD number for the patient is required to be unique across a particular hospital, city, or state, such as the state of Maharashtra, it follows the standard nomenclature.

    The MRD number helps you search the Amrita HMIS system for a specific patient and to access the Electronic Medical Record (EMR) of the patient. It helps both patient and hospital keep track of patient medical records as patient changes hospitals or doctors over a period of time. An EMR contains complete information about a patient, including the patient’s name, address, diagnosis, lab tests, lab results, and so on.

    Victims of accidents or suicide attempts that are brought in unconscious or seriously wounded must be registered as Medico Legal Cases (MLC) cases.

    The Registration window helps enter patient information, register new patients, and generate an MRD number for the patient.

     

    NOTE: You must enter information for all mandatory fields marked with an asterisk (*), such as the first name of the patient, date of birth, age, gender, patient category and post office. The rest of the fields are optional.

     

    To register a new patient:

    1. Click on the AHMIS icon on your desktop to launch the AHMIS application. The Amrita HMIS web application appears.

    2. Click on the Registration menu and select the Registration-Encounter menu command. The New Visit & Revisit Registration window appears – having 2 tabs – Regis-tration & Encounter. Registration tab is open by default.

    3. In the Patient Particulars field, enter the new patient’s first name.

    4. Enter the date of birth, age, and gender of the patient.

    5. Optionally, enter other particulars of the patient, such as last name, Region, Blood Group, and Remarks.

    6. Enter the local address details of the employee such as house name/appt number, address, post office/town, country, state, district/city, and so on.

    7. Check the check box for Permanent Address if it same as the Local Address.

    8. Click Save to capture the patient details and assign a new MRD number.

    The AHMIS system saves the information, registers the patient, and creates a new MRD number for the patient. At the bottom of the window, it displays the message Registration completed successfully, for Patient Name (displays patient name); MRD Number (displays MRD number) along with the name of the patient and the MRD number.

    Note:

    To encounter casualty patients:

    Select Consultation Type as ER.

    To print the MRD card for a patient:

    Click Print MRD Card button on the Registration window to print the MRD card of the newly registered patient.
