    The need for setting ‘Credit Limit’

    In its day to day operations, a hospital caters to a large number of patients belonging to different sections of the population. The patients visits the hospital in its ‘OP’s with due appointments, use casualty/emergency care counters on emergency and gets admitted when needed. The patient population can be generally divided into ‘Self-Pay’ Patients and ‘Third Party Payers’ based on the financial commitments undertaken for their treatment.

    In both these cases hospitals need to keep a check on the financial commitments of its pa-tients in the form of advance paid and credit limits specified vis a vis the bill amount of services rendered to the patients in order to avoid unnecessary embarrassments in the form of bills running out of the patient commitment levels resulting in delayed recovery or non recovery of the bills from the payers. On the other side, this will help the hospital in preventing the patient inconvenience due to various financial clearance hurdles even after paying/getting the required authorizations well in advance caused only because of lack of proper communication between the hospital departments.

    Key Features

    The feature ‘Setting up of Credit Limit’ enables the hospital to set credit limits to its patients beyond which the software restricts its ordinary users from rendering any ser-vices to the patients with the facility of overriding privilege to enhance the credit limit to selected users. The system keeps the audit log for future references. The credit limit so set is over and above the ‘Third Party Payer’ approved commitments and ‘Advance Paid’ included in the visit.

    The features include

    1. Facility to capture treatment advance made by the payers.

    2. Facility to capture ‘Third Party Approved Amount’

    3. Setting Specific Credit Limit, with ability to specify the amount of credit allowed against patient ‘Visit’ including specifying the following ‘Credit Type’.

     No Credit

     Unlimited Credit

     Limited Credit

    The total of ‘Advance Paid’, ‘Amount approved by the Third Party Payer’ and the ‘Specific Credit Limit’ specified against the visit less the amount already used, (here in after called the ‘Patient Account Balance’ is termed as the ‘Overall Credit Limit’ of the patient for the visit.

    4. Setting separate Specific Credit Limit for ‘Pharmacy and Material’ issues with facility to specify the Credit Type. This is subject to the overall credit limit.

    5. Adding ‘Credit Remarks’ which will be displayed in the relevant screens.
