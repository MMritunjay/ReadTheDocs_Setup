    Bank Reconciliation

    The bank ledger maintained by the organization may not tally with the statement of the bank account given by the bank, even though it is supposed to be. This is because of many reasons. Some of them are Cheques issued but not presented for payment, cheque deposited but not credited by the bank, Bank Charges debited by the bank, Direct credit to bank, data entry errors etc:- To arrive at the correct funds balance, a reconciliation of the bank ledger with the statement given by the bank is required. During this process all the issues mentioned above are identified and rectified, there by, at the end of the reconciliation, the users can arrive at the correct balance available in the bank. Amrita FIIS provides manual as well as automated ‘Bank Reconciliation’ features to the users.

    1. Steps to be followed for manual bank reconciliation using Amrita FIIS.

    1. Select ‘Bank Reconciliation’ sub-menu from the ‘Masters’ main-menu

    2. Enter ‘From date’ and ‘To date’ in the respective fields

    3. Select the Bank Account from the drop down against the field ‘Bank A/C

    4. Enter the amount in the amount column

    5. Click ‘Continue’; you will be directed to another page where you have a tabbed interface with Excel like pages

    6. Click on the tab ‘Get Ledger’; the ledger for the selected period can be seen with the relevant details in the earlier said ‘excel’ like page. An additional field ‘Reconciled’ can also be seen. By default the value will be ‘No’. At this point if you click on the Reconciliation tab, you will find that all the entries in the bank ledger will be either in the ‘Entries to be added to bank statement’ or in the ‘Entries to be deducted from the bank statement’. The difference amount will not be ‘Zero’. The ‘Reconcile’ button will be in an inactive state.

    7. Compare the details seen here with the details you have in the bank statement line by line. Find matching entries, if any, between the bank ledger and bank statement.

    8. Mark Reconciled status as ‘Yes’ against the matched entries

    Once the difference amount shown at the end of the ‘Reconciliation Statement’ becomes ‘0’, the ‘Reconcile’ button becomes active

    9. Click ‘Reconcile’; the reconciled entries will be updated to the data base.

    Reconciled entries will not appear again when the reconciliation process is repeated.

    2. Steps to be followed for automated bank reconciliation using Amrita FIIS.

    Amrita FIIS provides automated bank reconciliation feature also where by users can upload bank statements directly to the FIIS. The system will find the matching entries for you. The mismatches can be found out and reconciled manually. This helps in reducing the time spend for reconciling. It should be noted that the bank statement obtained from the bank should be in the standard format as prescribed by Amrita. The steps to be followed are as follows.

    1. Select ‘Bank Reconciliation’ sub-menu from the ‘Masters’ main-menu

    2. Enter ‘From date’ and ‘To date’ in the respective fields

    3. Select the Bank Account from the drop down against the field ‘Bank A/C

    4. Enter the amount in the amount column

    5. Use ‘Browse’ button and upload the bank statement to the FIIS; click ‘Continue’

    6. Click ‘Continue’; you will be directed to another page where you have a tabbed interface with Excel like pages

    7. Click on the tab ‘Get Ledger’; the ledger for the selected period can be seen with the relevant details in the earlier said ‘excel’ like page. An additional field ‘Reconciled’ can also be seen. By default the value will be ‘No’.

    8. Use ‘Auto-Reconcile’ button.

    9. Status of entries that are reconciled will be turned to ‘Yes’ in the ‘Reconciled’ field.

    10. Use ‘Add Adjustments’ if any entries are to be made to the bank ledger after verifying with the Bank statement. For Ex:- Commission, bank charges, Cheques dishonoured etc:-

    11. Change Reconciled status as ‘Yes’ against the matched entries

    Once the difference amount shown at the end of the ‘Reconciliation Statement’ becomes ‘0’, the ‘Reconcile’ button becomes active

    12. Click ‘Reconcile’; the reconciled entries will be updated to the data base.

    Reconciled entries will not appear again when the reconciliation process is repeated.
